\section{Introduction}

The Standard Model of particle physics contains 19 free parameters that must be measured experimentally. Among these, the masses of fundamental particles and their ratios remain unexplained---they are inputs, not outputs, of the theory. The Koide formula \cite{koide1982} observes that lepton masses satisfy $K = (m_e + m_\mu + m_\tau)/(\sqrt{m_e} + \sqrt{m_\mu} + \sqrt{m_\tau})^2 = 2/3$ to remarkable precision, yet the Standard Model offers no explanation.

We present the Genesis Engine, a framework that derives 90 physical quantities from a single geometric primitive:
\begin{equation}
    \Gamma = \frac{1}{6\varphi} \approx 0.1030056648
\end{equation}
where $\varphi = (1+\sqrt{5})/2$ is the golden ratio. This ``Gateway constant'' achieves:
\begin{itemize}

    \item \textbf{Neural stability}: First stable ``unbinding'' in self-modifying networks

This work builds on Dan Winter's phase conjugate physics \cite{winter2013} and Mark Rohrbaugh's mass equations \cite{rohrbaugh2015}, extending their insights to a complete 90-formula catalog with cross-domain validation.
